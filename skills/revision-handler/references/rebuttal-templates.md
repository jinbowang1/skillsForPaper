# Rebuttal Letter Templates

## Template 1: Standard Response to Reviewers

```
Dear Editor and Reviewers,

Thank you for your constructive comments and suggestions on our manuscript
entitled "[TITLE]" (Manuscript ID: [ID]).

We have carefully considered all the comments and have revised the manuscript
accordingly. Below, we provide point-by-point responses to each comment.
The reviewer comments are in **bold**, and our responses follow.
Changes in the manuscript are highlighted in [blue/red].

---

## Reviewer #1

**Comment 1.1:** [Reviewer's comment]

**Response:** [Your response]

**Changes:** [Description of changes made, with page/line numbers]

---

## Reviewer #2

...

---

We believe that the revised manuscript has been significantly improved
and hope that it now meets the standards of [JOURNAL NAME].

Sincerely,
[AUTHORS]
```

## Template 2: Disagreeing with a Reviewer (Politely)

```
**Comment X.Y:** [Reviewer's suggestion]

**Response:** We appreciate the reviewer's insightful comment. After careful
consideration, we respectfully maintain our original approach for the
following reasons:

1. [Reason 1 with evidence/citation]
2. [Reason 2 with evidence/citation]

However, we have added a discussion of this alternative perspective
in Section X (Page Y, Lines Z-W) to acknowledge the reviewer's valid point.
```

## Template 3: Acknowledging a Limitation

```
**Comment X.Y:** [Reviewer points out a limitation]

**Response:** We thank the reviewer for raising this important point.
We acknowledge this as a limitation of our current study. We have:

1. Added a discussion of this limitation in Section X.Y (Page Z, Lines W-V)
2. Suggested this as a direction for future work in the Conclusion section

While this limitation exists, we believe our main conclusions remain valid
because [reasoning].
```

## Template 4: Adding New Experiments/Analysis

```
**Comment X.Y:** [Reviewer requests additional experiments]

**Response:** Following the reviewer's excellent suggestion, we have
conducted [additional experiment/analysis]. The results are presented
in [new Figure/Table X] and discussed in Section Y.Z (Page W, Lines V-U).

The new results [support/complement] our original findings and show that
[key finding from new analysis].
```
