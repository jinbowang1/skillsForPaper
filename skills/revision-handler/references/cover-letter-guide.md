# Cover Letter Guide

## Initial Submission Cover Letter Template

```
Dear Editor,

We would like to submit our manuscript entitled "[TITLE]" for consideration
for publication in [JOURNAL NAME].

[1-2 sentences: Research background and motivation]

[2-3 sentences: Key findings and contributions]

[1 sentence: Why this journal is appropriate]

This manuscript has not been published and is not under consideration
for publication elsewhere. All authors have read and approved the
manuscript and agree with its submission to [JOURNAL NAME].

We suggest the following potential reviewers:
1. [Name, Affiliation, Email] — Expert in [relevant area]
2. [Name, Affiliation, Email] — Expert in [relevant area]
3. [Name, Affiliation, Email] — Expert in [relevant area]

[Optional: Reviewers to exclude and reasons]

We declare no conflicts of interest.

Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,
[Corresponding Author Name]
[Affiliation]
[Email]
[ORCID]
```

## Revision Cover Letter Template

```
Dear Editor,

Thank you for the opportunity to revise our manuscript entitled "[TITLE]"
(Manuscript ID: [ID]).

We have carefully addressed all the comments from the reviewers and have
made substantial revisions to the manuscript. A detailed point-by-point
response to the reviewers' comments is provided in the attached
Response to Reviewers document.

Major changes include:
1. [Summary of major change 1]
2. [Summary of major change 2]
3. [Summary of major change 3]

All changes are highlighted in [blue/red] in the revised manuscript
for easy identification.

We believe the revised manuscript has been significantly improved and
hope it now meets the standards for publication in [JOURNAL NAME].

Sincerely,
[Corresponding Author Name]
```

## Key Principles

1. **Be concise** — Editors read many cover letters; keep it to one page
2. **Highlight novelty** — What makes your work new and important?
3. **Match the journal** — Show you understand the journal's scope
4. **Be professional** — Formal tone, no grammatical errors
5. **Suggest reviewers** — Shows you understand the field
6. **Disclose conflicts** — Transparency builds trust
